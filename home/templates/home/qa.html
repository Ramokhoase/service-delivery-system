Frequently asked questions about CityMenderSA

CityMenderSA is South Africa's first no-registration, interactive map, free public civic reporting platform that allows residents to report and track local infrastructure issues such as broken streetlights, potholes, water leaks, and sanitation problems. It is a citizen tool — we do not fix issues, but provide a clear and accessible way to document and track them.
No. CityMenderSA does not fix or resolve any reported issues. We serve as a community-driven tracking tool. You are still responsible for reporting the issue to your municipality or service provider. You can use your municipal reference number when submitting your CityMenderSA report to link both.
Because CityMenderSA helps you:
  • Track and update your reported issues in one place
  • Keep a personal record and history of your submissions
  • View all community reports on an interactive map
  • Identify trends and hotspots (e.g. frequent water pipe bursts, neglected areas)
  • Create a public record that strengthens accountability and transparency
CityMenderSA also benefits municipalities and ward councillors by providing a centralised view of reported issues, helping prioritise high-impact areas, and supporting data-driven planning.
Yes. CityMenderSA is 100% free for public use. No subscriptions, no accounts needed.
No. You don't need to log in or register to use CityMenderSA. It's designed to be quick, simple, and accessible to everyone.
Currently, we support reports under the following categories:
  • Streetlights (e.g. broken fittings, poles, cable theft, lights on during the day)
  • Water Leaks (e.g. pipe bursts, meter damage)
  • Waste & Sanitation (e.g. blocked drains, sewerage spills, illegal dumping)
  • Roads & Potholes (e.g. potholes, sinkholes, faded markings, broken signs)
More categories may be added in future updates.
